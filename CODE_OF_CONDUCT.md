# Community Code Of Conduct

MarketWitness welcomes contributors who improve public-source research,
methodology, documentation, accessibility, localization, testing and
responsible data governance.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, specific and constructive in issues, discussions, reviews and
  contributed evidence.
- Critique claims, methods and source rights with evidence rather than
  attacking people.
- Disclose uncertainty, conflicts of interest and data limitations when they
  matter to a proposal.
- Respect privacy, source terms and the boundary between research evidence and
  investment advice.
- Help make the project accessible to contributors from different markets,
  languages and levels of experience.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, personal attacks or unwanted contact.
- Publishing private information, credentials, personal contact headers or
  restricted datasets.
- Promoting manipulated results, undisclosed marketing, market manipulation or
  claims presented as guaranteed financial outcomes.
- Repeated disruption of discussions or refusal to follow documented evidence
  and publication controls.

## Scope And Enforcement

This code applies in repository issues, discussions, pull requests and other
official MarketWitness community spaces. Maintainers may edit or remove
content, close interactions, or restrict participation when needed to protect
the project and its community.

For sensitive reports, use GitHub private vulnerability reporting as documented
in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). For ordinary conduct concerns that do not expose
private information, open a discussion addressed to the maintainers.
